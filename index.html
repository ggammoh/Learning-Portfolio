In the summer of 2022, I was given the opportunity to spend 3 weeks at Carnegie Mellon University participating in a Program called AI4ALL. Different university's programs can vary in length and rigor; however, the overall goal of AI4ALL is to equip students from underrepresented backgrounds the necessary knowledge and skills to educate others about AI and explore the field for themselves.

I got to learn about Concepts such as Convolutional Neural Networks, Gradient Descent, and Feature Learning while engaging with a team of 3 other hard-working students to create a computer vison model with the purpose of identifying 7 different types of cancerous skin lesions.

Over the course of the 3 week I also had the opportunity to attend daily lectures from various CMU professors to learn from and discuss with about their research and the diverse fields that artificial intelligence can be applied to.
Even though the program was only a few short weeks, I was able to make so many deep friendships that have both inspired me academically, and taught me so much about the diverse world around us.
